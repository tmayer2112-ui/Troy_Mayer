Electromechanical Optimization of Dielectric Gels

Chortos Lab | Soft Robotics Research

Project Summary

Maximized the electromechanical force output of ionic dielectric gels for soft robotic applications through targeted chemical formulation and custom automated testing.

My Role

Undergraduate Researcher driving the gel synthesis process and independently developing custom computer vision software for advanced data acquisition.

Process & Solution

Iteratively formulated new gel variations and designed 15 custom Fusion 360 test fixtures. To solve the challenge of accurately capturing sub-millimeter material behavior, I developed a Python-based computer vision autotracker, paired with LabVIEW and MATLAB, to autonomously quantify material displacement.

Impact & Metrics

Achieved a force output increase of over 50%. The tracking pipeline autonomously processed datasets exceeding 50,000 points, achieving 0.01 mm tracking precision on a 29.45 mm cantilever sample.

Top Skills Utilized
Python Computer Vision
LabVIEW & MATLAB
Fusion 360 CAD
DIW 3D Printing

Actuation modes of dielectric elastomers driving the research.

Macro-photography of a 29.45 mm dielectric gel cantilever under actuation. The micrometer scale was utilized by the Python autotracker to quantify material displacement.

Custom LabVIEW block diagram engineered to autonomously acquire and synchronize high-frequency data from the physical test fixtures.

MATLAB analysis of gel displacement over time, comparing mechanical response at varying spin-coating speeds.

Force magnitude testing under varying voltage loads utilized to optimize the chemical composition.
